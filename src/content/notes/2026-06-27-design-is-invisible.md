Great interfaces are usually the ones users never notice. The best UX disappears into the experience itself.

I realized this while testing DeshKaRojgar with first-time users. Nobody complimented the design. They just found jobs. That's when I understood — invisible design is successful design.

The moment someone says "nice UI" instead of completing their task, something went wrong. The interface should be a window, not a painting.

Good design is like good infrastructure. Nobody notices the roads until there's a pothole.
