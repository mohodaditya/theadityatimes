Waiting for perfection often delays valuable feedback. Ship early, learn fast, iterate always.

I spent three weeks polishing a feature nobody asked for. When I finally shipped it, users wanted something completely different. The lesson was painful but permanent.

Perfection is the enemy of progress. The best code is the code that reaches users.

Every day you don't ship is a day you don't learn.
